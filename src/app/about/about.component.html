About the database

Identity Labels

Three different levels of 'explicitness' are assigned to characters. These describe how the a-spec elements of the character are portrayed in the work. These are:

  1. Word Used - a specific word indicating their a-spec identity is used in the text of the work.
  2. Described - a word is not used, but the identity is described sufficiently to be understandable as an a-spec one.
  3. Other Works - neither word nor description is included in this work, but the character appears in other works where they are named or described.

In the case where different parts of the character's identity (eg. romantic and sexual orientation) are described at different levels of explicitness, the most explicit will be used. eg. If the word asexual is used, but an aromantic identity is implied from the description, the 'Word Used' explicitness will be given.

Additionally, characters may use multiple labels, such as identifying with an umbrella label and a microlabel. When this is the case, the more specific label will be used. eg. If a character describes themself as both aromantic and lithromantic, lithromantic will be used.

Intersectionalities

It is also possible to filter works on various intersectionalities. These will select works where an a-spec character in the work identifiably also belongs to one of the marginalised groups listed. It will not include cases where non-a-spec characters in the work belongs to one of these groups. The use of the person of colour label is based on a limited framework that doesn't make sense in most parts of the world. However it is useful in the context of short stories sourced from authors living in western and white majority countries to highlight ethnicities that don't get as much representation.

Length

While this database is focused on short stories, short fiction which can go unnoticed can vary in length. To highlight the works which vary from the short story length, a badge is placed on the listing. These are:

- Novella ~ 17,500 - 40,000 words

- Novelette ~ 7,000 - 17,000 words

- Flash Fiction ~ < 1,000 words

- Poem

Other Aromantic and Asexual Story Databases

There are a number of other databases where stories with aromantic and asexual spectrum characters can be found. These are listed below.

The AroAce Database is great for finding a-spec stories mostly from around 2021 or before. It also contains a lot of more obscure indie/self published books that are not easily recorded in other databases and lists. It's character based, although you can easily find information about what books each character appears in, what genre those books are, the most important type of relationship for each character, and any particular notes and warnings.

The aro & ace book database is great for finding more recent books and for searching for particular types of representation. This database is book/series based, and you can filter by format, genre, identity, importance of representation to the story, and main/side characters. The creator of the database has read all of the books on it and is still actively adding books to it as of February 2024. In addition, they leave a reasonably detailed description for each entry about the representation in it.

A page listing various free to read fiction available online with aromantic and asexual characters. These include details of the aro/ace characters and what relationships they have, information on the type of work, and includes some 'anti-recommendations'.
